Slips vs. Relapses: Understanding the Difference

There is a common and incorrect understanding in the recovery journey from pornography and its effects, revolving around the concepts of slips and relapses.

What is a Slip?

A slip is a minor, accidental event, not a full-blown relapse into excessive pornography use. Slips:

  • Are a natural part of the recovery journey.
  • Have less severe negative consequences compared to relapses.

What is a Relapse?

A relapse starts with a slip but continues over a longer period—perhaps several days—where the individual gives in and returns to old habits. Relapses:

  • Have a harmful impact on recovery plans and mental and physical well-being.
  • Can also affect one's psychological state.

Turning Setbacks into Successes

During your recovery journey, you will experience both victories and setbacks. Use those setbacks as fuel to achieve greater success and accomplishment in your recovery.

“What doesn’t kill you makes you stronger.”

How to Learn from Slips

Learn how to document your experiences. Understand that a relapse often stems from a slip. The key to preventing a slip from becoming a relapse is to:

  • Learn from the slip and avoid the triggers that led to it.
  • Immediately after a slip, be honest with yourself. Write down:
    • Your state before the slip occurred.
    • Your daily routine on that day.
    • The factors that led you to slip again.
  • By identifying these factors, you can become stronger and better equipped to handle triggers that lead to slips and prevent them from becoming relapses.

Understanding the Consequences of Relapses

Relapses often result from a disruption in lifestyle, causing the individual to escape reality into pornography. The effects of a relapse include:

  • Feelings of depression, frustration, anxiety, and anger—emotions the person was trying to escape through pornography.
  • Sometimes, after a relapse, individuals may even contemplate suicide.

Key Differences

A slip is a temporary setback and usually happens once. A relapse is a repeated return to previous behaviors over an extended period.

Do not handle the definitions of slips and relapses incorrectly! Address your slip before it leads to a relapse and avoid falling back into the dark tunnel of pornography. Learn from it to improve your recovery plan.

No matter how many slips you experience, do not despair. Quickly get back on track and continue your recovery journey. Fill the void left by pornography with worship, exercise, or hobbies. Work on developing yourself in all areas of your life.
